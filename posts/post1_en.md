Recently, I harbor a vague sensation within that my personality seems to be undergoing an enlightenment—for lack of a better word—unprecedented in my brief twenty years of existence.

After breaking away from exam-oriented education, I seem to be experiencing **"the unbearable lightness of being."** Upon realizing that there is no longer a powerful institution to constrain my actions within a specific time and space, that bottoming-out sense of liberation followed, yet it also brought immense emptiness and confusion. Up until the beginning of this summer, I still had not shaken off the "institutionalized student mentality," and this continuous self-conflict made me feel profoundly miserable.

Like most peers, I invested all my free time in video games and short videos; if there was any deviation, it was cryptocurrency contract trading.

The turning point occurred this summer. I attempted to explore the truly meaningful "self," locked myself in the dormitory for two weeks, and conducted a **self-"Zen meditation,"** severing all social interactions: 
Wake up, first watch two hours of movies I had previously missed; at noon, get takeout (mainly Baozaihua ginger chicken claypot rice); continue watching movies for three hours in the afternoon; at six, after half an hour of core workout, embark on a 30km cycling trip around Xiaoguwei; go eat a bowl of noodles, sleep.

The terminus of this trial was my solo cycling journey from Guangzhou, through Dongguan, to Shenzhen's Bao'an. But the true inflection point happened when I abandoned my laptop to travel light. After returning to Shenzhen, abstaining from video games plunged me into extreme boredom once again. I pondered during sleepless nights, conversed with AI, and after massive self-reflection and communication, that singularity of qualitative change finally arrived—**I chose to distill my past life's methodologies of dealing with people and things into written, systematic principles.**

---

### 01 / Why Not Give It a Try?

Besides money, there are no other factors that can impede my continuous exploration, both outward and inward. I began to realize: **Life is an experience.**

Those thoughts that once made me shrink back are now all intercepted by this principle. Yes, this is my primary philosophy of life, prompting me to think whenever I have any potential idea: why not try it? Frankly speaking, after establishing this first principle, my former cowardice began to dissipate.

### 02 / Spiritual Freedom

The freedom mentioned here, regarding my internal state, means I begin to **joyfully accept every thought in my mind**, whether beautiful and kind, or ugly and evil.

This self-acceptance ensures I no longer waste time navigating internal friction. I firmly believe that every unique thought is justified, and it is precisely the projection of these thoughts that grants me the possibility to continuously reflect and self-correct.

Regarding others, I realize our life experiences can never be identical, making the homogenization of everyone's spiritual thoughts an absurdity. I begin to comprehend and respect every viewpoint, value system, political stance, or sexual orientation and gender identity. **Rather than confronting them, it is superior to dismantle prejudices and exchange life experiences.** Similarly, I will maintain and defend everyone's right to freedom of speech until my death. Without freedom of speech, genuine thinking and communication are nonexistent.

### 03 / Altruism

The first time I encountered this concept was from a YouTube creator, HackBear Terry. In his video "Why are you unhappy," he raised this point: **lower material desires, learn to treat others well.**

This profoundly inspired me. I started attempting to lend a helping hand to people around me periodically. Perhaps there is a trace of selfishness, but the reality is I always derive a significant sense of achievement when assisting others—a satisfaction I rarely obtain in other aspects of life.

### 04 / Learn to Demystify

The demystification here, beyond its literal interpretation, is intended to express **the cultivation of empathy**.

I once provided this example when discussing the topic with a close friend: when walking on the street, there are always strangers approaching. In the past, I subconsciously avoided eye contact and looked down at my phone. Later, I tried observing others' reactions as they walked past me—most people looked down at their phones, while a few made eye contact with me.

I began to realize: **No one in public spaces cares about your non-transgressive behavior.**

A more intuitive example: when someone else is delivering a speech on stage, 80% of the time I am absent-minded or looking at my phone; from another perspective, when I am giving a speech on stage, do I also not need to care excessively about my performance? This granted my posture and mentality immense encouragement. 

Learn to demystify others; all men are created equal. Perhaps your "idol" is merely an ordinary upright ape like yourself. **Do not deify others; it will only impede self-evolution.**

---

These four viewpoints constitute the foundation for constructing my life. There are some minor principles I will leave to document in future articles. Writing down this text serves, on one hand, as a summary of my past life, leaving a record for my future; on the other hand, I hope to offer everyone more dimensions for reflection.

Wishing you a pleasant life, until the next article.
